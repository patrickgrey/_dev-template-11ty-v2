The Structured OJTI Session / 

Introducing the structured session

In this section, we are going to cover how to deliver a structured OJT session. All OJT sessions contain the following elements, and will be covered first in this section:

  1. Preparing to deliver the session
  2. Briefing
  3. Monitoring and Intervention (excluding taking over control)
  4. Debriefing

The above elements are likely to happen in all OJT sessions and will include things like questioning techniques and notetaking. However, there are some elements that only happen or are needed from time to time. We will look at these less routine situations next. They include techniques that you may call on in specific training situations and include:

  • Doing a demonstration, and/or
  • Using the trainee involvement technique
  • Doing a trainee talk through

This will be followed by some considerations for the OJTI when doing a:

  • Working position handover with your trainee.

Although not routine (hopefully!) it may be necessary, during a session to:

  • Take over control from your trainee

To help consolidate this learning, you will be able to watch a series of videos that demonstrate each element. In these videos you will be introduced to Dragan, one of the OJT instructors and Carla, the trainee.
