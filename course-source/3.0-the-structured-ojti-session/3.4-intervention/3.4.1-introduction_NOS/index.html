The Structured OJTI Session / Intervention 

Introduction

During training, situations will arise where you need to intervene. These interventions will disrupt the trainee's thought processes and often lead to changes in the sequences of actions the trainee carries out. Bear in mind that for the trainee, any intervention will cause the trainee to mentally pause what they are doing or the plan they are carrying out, listen to what you are saying, understand what is required of them, and then act.

When to intervene?

As the instructor, you decide when and for what reason to intervene, e.g. point of safety, demonstration, error correction or instruction to reduce workload.

This decision is influenced by issues such as:

  • Knowledge of and relationship with trainee

    In the early stages of the OJTI/Trainee relationship you may not know how competent the trainee is, or be unsure of their work style or weak areas and may choose to intervene sooner.

  • Your mental and physical wellbeing

    Instructing is a demanding task and there are likely to be days when you recognise that due to tiredness or other factors, you are not performing optimally. In this situation you may choose to intervene earlier to ensure that you maintain a safe environment.

  • Level of complexity

    The traffic density and complexity may cause the trainee's workload to go above what was stated in the T/O's and you may decide that it will be unproductive, or even detrimental, to try and let the trainee work continue.

There are no limits on when intervention could be needed. New situations are always cropping up and this is likely to continue as levels of traffic and complexity increase. Constantly changing operational conditions are something that we all have to live with. It is important to remain vigilant and aware that you may need to step in at a moment's notice - it may even be for a situation you yourself may not have come across before.
