The Structured OJTI Session / 

Trainee Involvement

There will be times, planned or otherwise; that you will involve your trainee in the early stages of OJT by delegating a particular task. This can be done for various reasons:

  1. You have given a demonstration and now you wish to check comprehension and understanding for a particular aspect, e.g. strip marking.
  2. You may have had to take control during a training session and before you hand back fully, you want to "ease" your trainee back into working by delegating a task and asking questions.
  3. In the early stages of OJT, or when the trainee is experiencing a particular problem, you may reduce their workload and get them to focus on a particular task they are struggling with to help build confidence.

Definition:

A method used by an OJTI to control the traffic, while engaging a trainee through a continuous exchange of ideas and information, plus the delegation of some tasks.

The benefits of this technique are:

  • It allows you to assess whether trainee has been listening
  • The trainee becomes involved in the decision making process
  • Weaknesses can be identified
  • It shows up inattention by a trainee to safety issues
  • You retain full control until you have confidence in the trainee

Involvement technique

Ask probing questions related to the work being done, but be careful to always keep in mind the following:

  • The trainee will think slower than you
  • Repeat important points, they may not be comprehended fully at first
  • Concentrate on safety as the main teaching point
  • Allow trainee to ask questions, subject to traffic and R/T load

When you are confident that the trainee has the picture, they can take control of a delegated task but you still retain full control. You could, for example, ask the trainee if they are ready to do the R/T, or alternatively take over the strip-marking but make sure you monitor their actions!
