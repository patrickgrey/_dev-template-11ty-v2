The Structured OJTI Session / 

Working position handover / takeover

An aspect of the OJT process that requires total focus on the part of the Instructor is during the Handover / Takeover of a working position by a trainee. If the trainee is taking over from the off-going ATCO, and depending on the working position and the HMI, it may be that the Instructor cannot plug her/his headset into the working position until the off-going ATCO has removed their headset plug. During this time, the OJTI does not get exactly the same "traffic picture" as the trainee if it is not possible to hear the transmissions made by aircraft on the frequency.

When the traffic levels warrant it, you should ensure that, before the trainee takes over the control of the position, you know what they plan to do. Consider these questions:

What will be your first three transmissions?
(Checks prioritisation)

What is your plan with the DLH1234 and SWR4321?
(If action is needed soon)

The OJTI needs to ensure that they are in a position to hear all the information the off-going ATCO passes to their trainee and to check it is complete. Only when the OJTI is satisfied can the Handover be accepted.

It has been documented that many incidents in ATC happen within the first five minutes of a Handover / Takeover due to various reasons. Incomplete transfer of all essential information, hurried Handover and the ATCO taking over not fully understanding the traffic situation.

Many organisations have developed acronyms to endeavour to ensure all relevant information is passed from the off-going to the ATCO taking-over. NATS UK developed "WEST" which is displayed on the mouse pads at the working position if relevant.



Weather: CBs, turbulence; low visibility or snow affecting airfields



Equipment: any unserviceability either planned or unplanned that will affect operations on the sector or adjacent sectors



Situation: flow regulations, military activity (danger areas, refuelling, non-deviating), conditional route availability, special flights (photo survey etc.)



Traffic: current and short term traffic situation including potential conflictions
