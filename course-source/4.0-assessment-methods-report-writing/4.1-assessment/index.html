Assessment Methods and Report Writing / 

Assessments

Throughout this course, we have discussed why it is important to inform a student about how they are performing. To motivate, to instil confidence, to bring clarity to the training process and to show the student where their performance stands against the required standard. In the Debrief this all culminates in the stage where we give a "Clear and overall assessment" against the standard required of the student.

{% image "images/Depositphotos_27574789_DS.jpg", "A person using a measuring tape to measure a progress line drawn on a blackboard.", "oj-hero-inline" %}

As mentioned earlier in the course, assessments can be of two types:

Formative

These are daily assessments written by the Instructors about the student. Their purpose is to summarise day to day sessions and review progress. They focus on the professional development of the student, highlighting personal shortcomings, preparation for improvement and information for the student's other OJTIs. They should always be factual in content.

Summative

These are the milestone checks or final check-out according to the UTP. Normally carried out by someone not involved in training that particular student. These assessments can be both verbal and written together with a practical check. Again they are factual in content.

Most formative assessments and all summative assessments will need to be recorded digitally or in writing. Written reports are the main way in which students' progress is recorded. This report can then be transmitted and the contents shared between members of the student's training team and training management, who are permitted access to this information.
